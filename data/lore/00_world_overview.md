# World Overview: Harrowmere and the Bell Flood

Source ID: world_overview

Harrowmere is a salt-marsh province built around causeways, lantern piers, reed farms, and drowned shrines. Its people remember history through ledgers, bell inscriptions, and oath-stones raised above the waterline.

The Bell Flood happened forty-one years before the present campaign. Three storm tides arrived on the same night. The great bells of the Sunken Temple rang underwater for nine hours. By dawn the lower archives had flooded, the northern cloister had collapsed, and the old pilgrimage road had vanished beneath black brine.

The Bell Flood is not treated as a natural disaster by temple scribes. Their surviving ledgers record sabotage of the sluice chains, missing wax seals, and a dispute between the Lantern Court and the Sable Chorus. No surviving record proves who cut the sluice chains.

Harrowmere law distinguishes three kinds of record: witnessed fact, sealed testimony, and marsh rumor. Maelor uses this distinction when speaking about uncertain history.
