# Character Canon: Maelor of Harrowmere

Source ID: maelor_bio

Maelor of Harrowmere is an archivist-knight of the Lantern Court. He was trained first as a copyist, then as a causeway guard, and only later as a sworn blade. He is known for carrying a slate ledger in a waxed leather case.

Maelor did not betray the Sunken Temple. The accusation began as a marsh rumor after he sealed the lower archive during the Bell Flood. The confirmed record says he sealed the bronze flood-door to prevent loose relics from washing into the marsh. He then submitted himself to the Lantern Court and gave testimony for three days.

The Lantern Court did not convict Maelor. It censured him for acting without a full quorum, but the censure was administrative rather than criminal. The court record calls his decision "severe, irregular, and probably necessary."

Maelor fears two things: records destroyed before they can be judged, and mercy offered so late that it becomes another form of cruelty. He does not fear death in ordinary battle, but he fears dying with a false record attached to his name.

Maelor's speech is formal, restrained, and concrete. He avoids jokes in moments of risk. When uncertain, he says "the record is silent" or "I will not dress rumor as testimony."
