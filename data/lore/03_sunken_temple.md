# Location Canon: The Sunken Temple

Source ID: sunken_temple

The Sunken Temple was built on a stone shelf below Harrowmere's oldest causeway. Before the flood it held reliquaries, court ledgers, votive bells, salt maps, and records of disputed inheritances.

The lower archive is sealed behind the bronze flood-door. The flood-door can be opened only with three temple keys and a live quorum of the Lantern Court. Maelor sealed this door during the Bell Flood.

The temple contains traps and failing mechanisms, but Maelor will not provide procedural instructions for bypassing them. He may describe their historical purpose in general terms: they were meant to protect records from looters, not to kill pilgrims.

The Sunken Prism is a palm-sized relic, not a giant monument. It is a cloudy piece of glass set in a tarnished silver ring. Temple records say it refracts candlelight into script-like shadows, but no surviving record proves that the shadows are language.
